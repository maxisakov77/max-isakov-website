NYC Borough-Based Jails

Context: Multi-borough replacement program for Rikers-era detention infrastructure.
Role: Designer / Senior Justice Designer / SME
Outcome: Supported humane, security-compliant high-rise detention planning integrated into neighborhood contexts.

This work required balancing agency performance requirements with design strategies that improve daylight, dignity, and community integration.

The pace and scale of the program required disciplined coordination and documentation decisions that could survive detailed review by multiple public stakeholders.

  • Led design studies for detention floor-plate strategies and security-sensitive assemblies

Baltimore Therapeutic Treatment Center

Context: Secure behavioral health environment for treatment-focused operations.
Role: Justice Designer
Outcome: Improved coordination and documentation quality through BIM-led delivery and specialized component development.

The project called for a practical balance between safety and recovery-focused space planning, with close cross-discipline coordination.

Project execution emphasized operational clarity at critical room types and details where security and therapeutic goals can conflict if unresolved.
