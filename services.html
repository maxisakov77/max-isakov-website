Services

Three focused services for faster, code-compliant, and buildable project decisions.

Development Architecture

I support NYC developers and property owners from feasibility through construction documentation. The goal is a coordinated set of decisions that is financially and technically realistic before major downstream commitments.

Who This Is For

  • Developers pursuing multifamily, mixed-use, or adaptive reuse projects
  • Owners evaluating repositioning or expansion potential on constrained sites
  • Teams needing one accountable lead from early strategy through filing-ready sets

Core Deliverables

  • Zoning and code fit analysis tied to practical massing options
  • Schematic design packages aligned with entitlement and DOB strategy
  • Construction documentation with coordinated consultant integration

Typical Timeline and Outcome

Early feasibility and concept packages are typically defined in weeks, not months, with clear assumptions on FAR, envelope constraints, and implementation risk. Teams move forward with fewer revisions later in DD/CD.

City of Yes Feasibility

City of Yes introduces major upside, but only when zoning, affordability requirements, and site geometry are tested together. I provide decision-grade feasibility before acquisition or predevelopment spend accelerates.

Who This Is For

  • Acquisition teams evaluating whether UAP and parking changes materially improve value
  • Developers comparing scenarios across multiple parcels
  • Architects and owners needing rapid support on City of Yes interpretation

Core Deliverables

  • UAP scenario analysis with density, affordability, and envelope impacts
  • Massing studies translating zoning text into buildable form
  • Net-area and unit-yield ranges for financial modeling and go/no-go decisions

Typical Timeline and Outcome

Most feasibility studies are structured as a short sprint with clear assumptions, options, and a recommendation. Clients leave with a defendable basis for next-step investment decisions.

Justice and Institutional Advisory

I advise on facilities where security, operations, and therapeutic performance have to be resolved simultaneously. This includes detention, behavioral health, and related institutional typologies.

Who This Is For

  • Architecture teams entering or scaling justice and behavioral health work
  • Project leaders needing security-compliance rigor in design and detailing
  • Stakeholder groups aligning operational and design priorities

Core Deliverables

  • Security and compliance review of layouts, details, and key assemblies
  • Design QA focused on operational flow, safety, and therapeutic intent
  • Documentation and BIM support for specialized facility components

Typical Timeline and Outcome

Advisory is scoped to critical project phases so teams can address high-risk decisions early. For select projects, I collaborate with Peter C. Krasnow, FAIA when additional senior justice expertise is valuable.
