Services

MAX AEC LLC provides three focused service lines for faster, code-compliant, and buildable project decisions.

Development Architecture

Our team supports NYC developers and property owners from feasibility through construction documentation. The scope is structured to keep design, code, and delivery assumptions aligned before downstream commitments intensify.

Who This Is For

  • Developers pursuing multifamily, mixed-use, or adaptive reuse projects
  • Owners evaluating repositioning or expansion potential on constrained sites
  • Project teams requiring accountable coordination from strategy through filing-ready sets

Core Deliverables

  • Zoning and code fit analysis tied to practical massing options
  • Schematic design packages aligned with entitlement and DOB strategy
  • Construction documentation with coordinated consultant integration

Typical Timeline and Outcome

Early feasibility and concept packages are typically completed in focused sprints with clear assumptions on FAR, envelope constraints, and implementation risk.

City of Yes Feasibility

Our feasibility process translates City of Yes and UAP policy into decision-grade project scenarios. Analysis is structured to test zoning opportunity, affordability obligations, and envelope realities in one coordinated pass.

Who This Is For

  • Acquisition teams evaluating whether UAP and parking changes improve value
  • Developers comparing multiple parcels before predevelopment spend increases
  • Architects and owners needing support on active City of Yes interpretation

Core Deliverables

  • UAP scenario analysis with density, affordability, and envelope impacts
  • Massing studies that convert zoning text into buildable form options
  • Net-area and unit-yield ranges for financial modeling and go/no-go decisions

Typical Timeline and Outcome

Most studies are delivered as short-cycle decision packages with clear assumptions and recommendations for next-step design or entitlement action.

Justice and Institutional Advisory

MAX AEC LLC advises on facilities where security, operations, and therapeutic performance must be resolved together. Engagements include detention, behavioral health, and related institutional typologies.

Who This Is For

  • Architecture teams entering or scaling justice and behavioral health work
  • Project leaders requiring security-compliance rigor in design and detailing
  • Stakeholder groups aligning operational and design priorities

Core Deliverables

  • Security and compliance review of layouts, details, and key assemblies
  • Design QA focused on operational flow, safety, and therapeutic intent
  • Documentation and BIM support for specialized facility components

Typical Timeline and Outcome

Advisory scopes are phased around high-risk project milestones. For select programs, the firm partners with Peter C. Krasnow, FAIA as senior justice advisor.
