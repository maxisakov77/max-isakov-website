City of Yes Feasibility

I help NYC developers convert zoning change into actionable development decisions.

The Core Problem

City of Yes can increase project potential, but the upside is easy to overstate when FAR, envelope, affordability obligations, and financing assumptions are not tested together. Teams need clear answers quickly: what is truly buildable, what is compliant, and what should be pursued now.

My Feasibility Method

1

Constraint Baseline

Establish zoning district controls, lot conditions, and governing constraints that will drive real buildability.

2

UAP and Density Scenarios

Test bonus potential and affordability implications against practical massing and envelope limits.

3

Yield and Risk Readout

Translate scenarios into usable unit/count and area ranges with clear assumptions and decision risks.

4

Recommendation

Provide a go/no-go recommendation and next-step scope for design, filing, or acquisition decisions.

What You Receive

Buildable FAR Range

Not just theoretical maximums, but envelope-tested outcomes.

UAP Impact Summary

Clear tradeoffs between density, affordability requirements, and value.

Massing Direction

Option set suitable for internal investment and design alignment.

Action Plan

Specific next steps for entitlement, design progression, or hold decisions.

Who This Is For

  • Developers evaluating acquisition targets in NYC
  • Owners considering redevelopment or vertical expansion
  • Investment teams requiring a fast and defensible feasibility basis
  • Architectural teams needing City of Yes support on active projects
